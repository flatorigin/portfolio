# Open Questions for Counsel

## 1. Platform positioning

- Is the facilitator-only position sufficiently clear?
- Do any current UI labels imply endorsement, agency, brokerage, or legal participation?
- Is the current AI disclaimer narrow enough?

## 2. Disputes and liability

- Should the platform require arbitration?
- Should there be a class-action waiver?
- What venue/governing-law structure is best?
- What limitation-of-liability language should be used?
- What indemnity structure is appropriate for user-posted content and disputes?

## 3. DMCA / copyright

- What exact notice and counter-notice language should be published?
- Is the current designated contact approach sufficient?
- What repeat-infringer threshold is safest and practical?
- What takedown timing language should be used publicly?

## 4. Privacy and retention

- What retention periods should be published, if any?
- What should happen to messages, reports, and audit logs after deletion requests?
- Does the blocked-email-after-deletion policy need additional disclosure?
- Are there any state-specific or international disclosures that must be added now?

## 5. Verification claims

- Is `Credentials reviewed` acceptable, or should it be narrowed further?
- What exact review process must occur before that label is displayed?
- Are state-by-state contractor licensing disclaimers required?
- Should insurance review and license review be split into separate public labels?

## 6. Product claims and copy

Counsel should review these user-facing areas specifically:

- `/terms`
- `/privacy`
- `/copyright`
- contractor edit-profile verification copy
- public contractor profile badge copy
- any future marketing claims around trust, safety, or verification

## 7. Operational follow-up after counsel review

Expected implementation work after legal review may include:

- copy changes on policy pages
- revised badge labels
- revised moderation/takedown workflows
- retention settings or deletion changes
- additional admin logging or evidence-retention steps
- jurisdiction-specific warnings or disclosures
