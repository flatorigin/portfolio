# Platform Positioning

## Core platform role

FlatOrigin is a facilitator platform for home-improvement discovery, project
organization, portfolio publishing, bid exchange, and user-to-user
communication.

The platform is intended to help:

- homeowners publish job posts and private planning drafts
- contractors publish portfolios and receive inquiries
- both sides exchange messages and proposals

FlatOrigin is not intended to act as:

- the contractor performing the work
- the homeowner's agent
- a broker or insurer
- a guarantor of project outcome, pricing, licensing, insurance, or safety
- the legal counterparty to contractor-homeowner agreements

## On-platform vs off-platform

What happens on-platform:

- account creation
- profile publishing
- project posting
- portfolio browsing
- bid/proposal exchange
- messaging
- moderation/reporting
- optional AI drafting/organization assistance
- contractor document-review workflow

What does not currently happen on-platform:

- payments or escrow
- permit handling
- insurance underwriting
- contract execution with platform-drafted binding legal terms
- regulatory license validation through government databases

## Contract and transaction posture

The product currently treats bids and project responses as discussion and
proposal tools, not automatically binding contracts.

Current intended position:

- a bid is a proposal for review
- messaging is communication support
- dashboards help organization and workflow
- the final contract, scope, payment terms, schedule, and legal obligations
  remain the responsibility of the parties

## AI posture

AI is used as a drafting and organization helper. It may assist with:

- project summaries
- contractor profile text
- planning summaries
- possible solution paths
- job-posting draft preparation

AI output is intended to be informational only and should not be treated as:

- legal advice
- contract review
- permit guidance
- insurance advice
- price guarantees
- professional engineering or construction advice

## Verification posture

The product now uses a document-review model, not a broad validation claim.

Contractors may submit:

- license number
- license state/jurisdiction
- insurance provider
- insurance policy number
- insurance expiration date

The platform should describe this as staff review of submitted information, not
regulatory certification or endorsement.

Public badge language is now limited to:

- `Review pending`
- `Credentials reviewed`
- `Review expired`

Those badges should not be treated as promises of:

- current legal licensing in every jurisdiction
- active insurance coverage at the time of hire
- workmanship quality
- code compliance
- business reliability

## Claims to avoid unless counsel approves them

- "licensed contractor" as a platform-certified claim
- "verified contractor" without defined substantiation standards
- "fully vetted"
- "approved by FlatOrigin"
- "insured and verified" unless a documented workflow supports both claims
- any wording that implies FlatOrigin guarantees performance or legal compliance

## Questions for counsel

1. Is the current facilitator-only position clear enough?
2. Do current UI flows or copy imply agency, brokerage, or endorsement?
3. Should bid and messaging language be tightened further?
4. Is the AI disclaimer sufficiently narrow?
5. What exact wording should replace or supplement the current review badge copy?
