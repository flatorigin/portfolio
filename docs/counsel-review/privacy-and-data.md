# Privacy, Retention, and Account Lifecycle

## Current public policy surfaces

The frontend now exposes:

- `/privacy`
- `/terms`
- `/copyright`

These pages describe the platform's intended privacy, content, and copyright
handling posture prior to final legal review.

## Data categories currently handled

The platform currently stores or processes:

- account credentials and email addresses
- profile details and service areas
- public portfolio and project content
- homeowner planning drafts
- bids and job-posting drafts
- messages and attachments
- moderation reports
- admin audit logs
- contractor-submitted license/insurance review data
- account security and deletion state

## Public vs private behavior

Public-facing content may include:

- profiles the user publishes
- public projects/job posts
- selected public contact methods
- service-area information
- review badge state where applicable

Non-public or internal data may include:

- account email addresses not chosen for display
- private messages
- moderation records
- report details
- audit logs
- deactivation/deletion records
- submitted contractor documents/data under review

## Account lifecycle behavior

Current supported behavior includes:

- deactivate account
- delete account with password confirmation
- block reuse of deleted email addresses
- freeze accounts/profiles internally through admin tools

Deletion does not necessarily imply complete purging of all internal records.
Operationally, some records may remain for security, moderation, audit, abuse
prevention, repeat-infringer handling, or legal defense.

## Retention posture

Retention windows are not yet finalized and should be set with counsel.

The current platform behavior and policy copy support retaining limited records
where reasonably necessary for:

- moderation and abuse prevention
- auditability
- dispute support
- legal defense
- repeat-infringer tracking
- security review

## Open privacy decisions

1. Published retention periods for:
   - messages
   - moderation reports
   - audit logs
   - deleted-account records
   - copyright complaints
2. How deletion requests should be handled when legal hold or abuse review is active
3. Whether additional region-specific privacy disclosures are needed before launch
4. Whether any cookie/analytics notice must be added now

## Questions for counsel

1. What retention periods should be published, if any?
2. Should the site describe deletion as deletion, deactivation, anonymization, or a mixed model?
3. What minimum records should remain after account deletion?
4. Does the blocked-email policy need a more explicit legal basis statement?
5. Are additional state or international privacy disclosures needed at this stage?
